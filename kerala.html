Kerala, nestled along the southwestern Malabar Coast of India, is a paradise of natural beauty and cultural richness. Known for its emerald backwaters, palm-fringed beaches, misty hill stations, and vibrant traditions, Kerala offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure. Whether you're cruising through the tranquil backwaters of Alleppey, exploring spice plantations in Thekkady, or experiencing traditional Kathakali performances, every moment in Kerala is infused with magic and serenity.

Places to Visit

Alleppey Backwaters

Experience the tranquil network of lagoons, lakes, and canals by traditional houseboat. Alleppey, known as the "Venice of the East," offers one of Kerala's most serene experiences with its palm-fringed waterways and village life.

Munnar

This picturesque hill station is famous for its vast tea plantations, misty mountains, and diverse flora and fauna. A haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers, Munnar's rolling hills offer stunning vistas and trekking opportunities.

Fort Kochi

A historic coastal area with Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonial influences. Known for its iconic Chinese fishing nets, ancient churches, and vibrant art scene, Fort Kochi offers a fascinating glimpse into Kerala's multicultural past.

Wayanad

A rural district known for its spice plantations, wildlife sanctuaries, and prehistoric caves. The lush forests of Wayanad are home to diverse wildlife, tribal communities, and ancient rock carvings dating back to 6000 BCE.

Major Attractions

Onam Festival

Kerala's most important festival celebrates the mythical King Mahabali's annual homecoming with floral arrangements (pookalam), boat races, traditional dances, and elaborate feasts (Onam Sadhya).

Ayurveda Treatments

Kerala is renowned for authentic Ayurvedic healing traditions. The state's climate and abundance of medicinal plants make it ideal for traditional wellness therapies that have been practiced for thousands of years.

Kathakali Performance

This classical dance-drama features elaborate costumes, vivid makeup, and detailed gestures telling stories from Indian epics. The performances combine dance, music, and acting in a unique cultural experience.

The Kozhikkodan Halwa

A signature delicacy of Kerala, stands out for its unique preparation using coconut oil, a method that distinguishes it from the ghee-based halwas found elsewhere in India. Its long shelf life without refrigeration has made it a popular treat across generations.

1. How do I greet locals in Kerala?
Say "Namaskaram" with folded hands. Adding "Sughamano?" (Are you well?) is appreciated.
2. What local dishes should I definitely try?
Must-try dishes include Kerala Sadhya (feast on banana leaf), Appam with Stew, Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish), and Puttu with Kadala Curry.
3. What are respectful ways to explore Kerala's culture?
Dress modestly at temples, remove shoes before entering homes/temples, and use your right hand for giving/receiving items.
4. What are the Eco-Friendly activities in Kerala?
Try bamboo rafting in Periyar, organic farm visits in Wayanad, bird watching in Kumarakom, and cycling tours through villages.
5. Is Kerala food spicy?
Kerala cuisine uses many spices but isn't overwhelmingly hot. It's aromatic with coconut, curry leaves, and black pepper rather than chili-hot.
