Jaipur, India

Location: Rajasthan, India
Population: ~3.8M
Languages: Hindi, Rajasthani, English
Time Zone: IST (UTC+5:30)

Known as the "Pink City", Jaipur is a mix of royal heritage and vibrant street life ‚Äî but it also faces tourism-related environmental stress.

Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is famous for its pink-colored buildings, royal palaces, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites like the Amer Fort and Jantar Mantar. It is part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit and is rich in arts, crafts, textiles, and traditional Rajasthani cuisine. As tourism grows, so does the need for sustainable practices to preserve its historic charm and local livelihoods.

Places to Visit

Hawa Mahal

Iconic "Palace of Winds" with 953 small windows designed for royal women to observe street festivals unnoticed. A blend of history and breeze!

Amer Fort

Overlooking Maota Lake, this majestic fort is known for its artistic Hindu elements, elephant rides, and sound & light shows.

City Palace

Historic royal residence now partially converted into a museum. It features grand courtyards, gardens, and intricate architecture.

Jantar Mantar

World‚Äôs largest stone sundial and astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II. A scientific marvel of its time!

Albert Hall Museum

Oldest museum in Rajasthan with Indo-Saracenic architecture. Exhibits include ancient armor, paintings, sculptures, and more.
